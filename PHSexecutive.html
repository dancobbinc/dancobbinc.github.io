Presbyterian Healthcare Services

Supplemental Individual Disability Insurance Benefit

PHS automatically provides group long-term disability (LTD) insurance at no cost to the executives. The plan pays an income replacement benefit of 60% of base salary up to a maximum benefit of $15,000 per month. The benefit is payable in the event you become disabled and the disability continues beyond the 90 day elimination/waiting period. The benefit is taxable since PHS pays the premium. You must be benefit eligible to qualify for the coverage.

This is an excellent benefit, but as is typical, group plans often do not provide complete income protection. Due to the normal benefit limits and taxation of group disability insurance plans, a substantial portion of your income, which includes bonuses, retirement plan contributions, and other earned income, is not protected. Depending on your total income, supplemental individual disability insurance plans can offer additional income protection which, when combined with your group plan, can increase your overall protection up to 65% of your pre-disability income on a tax free basis. In addition, individual plans offer a variety of options that will allow you to choose a plan design in accordance with your particular needs. Your plan can be as comprehensive or as basic as you like and the cost is very affordable when the available permanent and portable discounts are considered.

Presbyterian has made several popular individual disability insurance plans available to you at a substantial premium discount through Dan Cobb and Associates. One of these plans offers a considerably lower cost unisex rate for female professionals. You must work at least 30 hours per week from all employment combined in order to qualify for these plans.

About Underwriting

Individual disability insurance is medically and financially underwritten and the insurance companies tend to be very thorough in this process. The more information I have up front, the better job I can do in helping you find the best plan and at the most competitive cost. Underwriting rules vary considerably and if there are health issues or unusual circumstances, it will be necessary to shop the market for the best deal. Any information that I obtain in a conversation with you is strictly confidential and is not shared or discussed with anyone except an insurance underwriter. Even then, I never use your name unless a formal application is submitted.

Disability insurance companies want your business, however, and will try to accommodate a variety of unfortunate circumstances. If necessary, they can modify a plan by charging a higher premium, declining to offer a particular option, or excluding certain medical issues in order to be able to offer a policy.

I know the sponsored companies are very good but I am insurance company and product neutral while working on your behalf. My goal is to make you happy with the outcome if an application is submitted.
